Collaborative Computation

Mathematicians are able to define problems and simply distribute computation to volunteers. Similar distributed computing projects have existed such as Folding at Home, Search for Extraterrestrial Life (SETI), and the Search for Mersenne Primes. Arithmetica provides a reusable framework allowing the computation of new and diverse problem sets.

Explore Data

Arithmetica data is published without restriction and is shared freely with the public. Computational mathematics data is often siloed, restricted, and unable to be resumed. Free decentralized access to this data is an important tenant and will provide mathematicians with empirical data to help prove theorems.

Share Knowledge

Scientific Journals are big business and limit access to a select few. Arithmetica will become a place for mathematicians to communicate, get feedback, and share their findings on the distributed web. This sharing of knowledge will do for the mathematical community what GitHub did for open source software and inspire the next generation of mathematicians.

Why it's Important

"Mathematics is not a deductive science - that’s a cliche. When you try to prove a theorem, you don’t just list the hypotheses, and start to reason. Odd Perfect Number Conjecture

Perfect numbers are positive integers that are the sum of their proper divisors. The smallest perfect number is 6, which is the sum of 1,2, and 3. Other perfect numbers include 28, 496, 8128. No one knows if there exists any odd perfect numbers.
